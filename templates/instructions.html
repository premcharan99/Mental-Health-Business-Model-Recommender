Instructions & Explanations

How It Works

This system predicts the best business model for a mental health service using a Random Forest model trained on 2,000 synthetic records. Follow these steps:

  1. Navigate to Dashboard: Select attributes like service type and budget using dropdowns.
  2. Submit: Click "Predict" to get the top 2–3 business models with confidence scores.
  3. View Results: See recommendations and visualizations (bar and pie charts) for clarity.
  4. Explore Dataset: Check the full dataset and download it.
  5. Understand Data: View dataset visualizations to see patterns.

Component Explanations

  • Dashboard: Input form for service details, displays predictions (e.g., Subscription 70%), and shows charts to visualize confidence.
  • Dataset: Table of 2,000 records (e.g., service type, business model) with a download button for analysis.
  • Visualizations: Bar chart of service types (e.g., 40% Outpatient) and pie chart of business models (e.g., ~16% each) to understand data distribution.
  • Instructions: This page, guiding usage and explaining components.

Attribute Information

  • Service Type: Type of service (e.g., Teletherapy). Influences model (e.g., apps → Freemium).
  • Ownership: Entity type (e.g., Private Nonprofit). Affects funding (e.g., grants → Nonprofit).
  • Budget: Budget in $K (5–500). Small for apps ($20K), large for inpatient ($300K).
  • Target Age Group: Clients (e.g., Young Adults). Impacts demand (e.g., Adults → high).
  • Location: Area (e.g., Urban US). Urban → high demand, rural → nonprofits.
  • Market Demand: Need level (e.g., High). High suits scalable models.
  • Delivery Mode: Method (e.g., Online). Online → apps.
  • Payment Methods: Cash, Insurance, Grants, Subscription, Free (Yes/No). Guide model (e.g., grants → Nonprofit).
  • Accessibility Goal: Focus on underserved (e.g., High). High → nonprofits.

Example

Input: Teletherapy, Private For-Profit, $50K, Young Adults, Urban Global, High Demand, Online, Cash+Subscription, Medium Accessibility.

Output: Subscription (70%), Freemium (20%), Pay-Per-Use (10%).

Why: Online, for-profit, and subscription suggest scalable revenue (e.g., BetterHelp).
